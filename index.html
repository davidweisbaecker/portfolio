About Me

From operational bottlenecks to strategic product decisions

Great products fail if the underlying processes don't scale. Before diving deep into UX Design and Product Management, I managed operational workflows and process design. That taught me a critical lesson: a seamless user interface is useless without a robust, scalable system architecture behind it.

Today, I translate complex business logic and user research into clear engineering requirements — making sure teams build the right features at the right time, with measurable outcomes.

How I Work

Beyond the toolkit

01

Translation between worlds

Running my own e-commerce business, I constantly balanced site performance against features — auditing load times, swapping out heavy plugins, compressing media and briefing freelance developers to slim down the code. My role has always been making different disciplines understand each other, turning fuzzy requirements into clear, actionable direction.

02

Clarity over complexity

I communicate uncertain or technical ideas through clear visuals and presentations — from designing an information poster for a university app to breaking down complex topics into simple graphics as a coach. If a stakeholder can't follow it, it isn't finished.

03

Meeting people where they are

As a coach and tutor, I never left anyone behind when their pace differed from the plan — I re-prioritised and adapted the content so each person still reached their goal. That same instinct drives how I shape a roadmap around a team's real constraints rather than an idealised one.

04

Calm when the plan breaks

When I'm wrong, I don't panic. I gather fresh input from research, AI and experts, salvage whatever is reusable and rebuild an efficient plan. Just as importantly, I keep the team's morale up while we course-correct — momentum matters as much as the fix.

Today I run a tutoring-matching business — managing relationships between parents and tutors, resolving conflicts and pairing the right teachers with the right students. Stakeholder management isn't theory for me; it's what I do every week.

Research Deep-Dive

Designing for the unpredictable

A deep-dive into Microsoft's 18 Guidelines for Human–AI Interaction (Amershi et al., 2019) — and what they mean for today's SaaS products.

For a university seminar I examined this landmark study in depth and presented its implications. My takeaway: AI is not a magic feature — it's a new UX material with its own rules. Probabilistic systems break classic usability principles, forcing product teams to design for error tolerance, latency trade-offs and user expectation management. It's the research that shaped how I think about AI products.
